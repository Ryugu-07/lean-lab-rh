## Locked primary-source mechanism

Groskin, [arXiv:2607.02828](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02828), Lemma 2.3 and
Theorem 2.5, fixes the following objects for centered frequencies `m=-N,...,N` and a real
reflection-even coefficient vector `u`:

```text
T_u(t) = sum_m u_m exp(2*pi*i*m*t),
K_u(omega) = 2 * integral_0^omega T_u(t) T_u(omega-t) dt,
psi_(alpha,omega)(x) = alpha/pi * sin(2*pi*omega*x).
```

For the divided-difference matrix `Q_(alpha,omega)`, including derivative samples on the
diagonal, the source claims

```text
u^T Q_(alpha,omega) u = alpha * K_u(omega).
```

It then defines, with `Delta=log(c)/(2*pi)`,

```text
hat(g_u)(xi) =
  pi * K_u(1-|xi|/Delta)  when |xi|<=Delta,
  0                       otherwise.
```

At a prime power `q<=c`, `xi=log(q)/(2*pi)` maps exactly to
`omega=1-log(q)/log(c)`, so the finite prime matrix becomes the prime side of the induced
explicit-formula test. The full theorem additionally needs admissibility, the pole and
archimedean transports, the cutoff-free archimedean limit, and the Guinand--Weil formula.

The project already compiles the centered-frequency matrix API and the literal finite von
Mangoldt source, but has no `T_u`, `K_u`, `hat(g_u)`, or source-calculus theorem.

## Why this can find an omission

The finite spectral matrices are often treated as numerical approximants. The dictionary's
stronger claim is that each finite quadratic value already evaluates an exact zeta-zero test
after a closed-form transport. Formalizing the transport distinguishes three possibilities:

1. the source normalization and diagonal match exactly, preserving the claimed bridge;
2. the atom calculation works but the cutoff/Fourier coordinate does not, exposing a source
   mismatch;
3. the finite bridge works while the real obstruction remains the missing inverse/density or
   source-limit theorem, sharply locating why finite positivity cannot yet imply RH.
