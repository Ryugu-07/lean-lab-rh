## Exact quantifiers and geometry

The endpoint has the form `forall T : Real, 0 <= T -> exists zeros : Finset Complex, ...`.
It records finiteness, nonemptiness, closure under both `star` and `rho |-> 1-rho`, open-strip
membership of every listed point, finite-height verification, and an existential witness
`rho` satisfying both `not OnCriticalLine rho` and `T < abs rho.im`.

For every member of the constructed orbit, `finiteHeightAuditOrbit_abs_im` proves the exact
identity `abs rho.im = T + 1`. Consequently, the verification predicate is true because its
height antecedent is impossible, while `finiteHeightAuditBase T` supplies an off-line point with
real part `1/4` strictly above the checked height. The result does not rely on selecting a special
numerical height.

## Exact theorem directions

`finiteHeightAuditOrbit_conj_closed` and `finiteHeightAuditOrbit_one_sub_closed` prove closure of
the explicit finite model under the two geometric operations. They do not prove that these are
the only structural constraints on an actual zeta zero set.

`finiteHeightAuditOrbit_inCriticalStrip` proves every model point lies in the open strip.
`finiteHeightAuditOrbit_verified` proves all model points through height `T` lie on the critical
line; in this construction there are no model points through that height.

`finiteHeightAuditOrbit_has_high_offLine` proves the complementary high witness. The aggregate
theorems `exists_finiteHeightAuditOrbit` and `finiteHeightPromotionAudit_endpoint` therefore
falsify only the source-independent inference from a finite certificate plus these two symmetries
to a global critical-line conclusion.
