## Source Audit

Baez-Duarte, *A Strengthening of the Nyman-Beurling Criterion for the Riemann Hypothesis*,
arXiv `math/0202141`, Theorem 1.1, states that RH is equivalent to membership of the indicator
`chi_(0,1)` in the `L2(0,infinity)` closure generated by the positive-natural kernels
`rho(1/(n*x))`. The paper obtains the reverse direction from inclusion in the full Beurling space
and the classical Nyman-Beurling criterion.

This formalization may prove the same necessity implication directly, without asserting the more
general base criterion. The direct argument is the standard Mellin zero obstruction already
implicit in the criterion: for `0 < Re(s) < 1`,

```text
Mellin(rho(1/(n*x)))(s) = n^(-s) * (-zeta(s)/s).
```

At a zeta zero with `1/2 < Re(s) < 1`, every finite natural-kernel sum has zero full Mellin
transform, while the target has transform `1/s`.

## Checked Route

For a source finite coefficient vector `c`, write `m` for its reciprocal moment. The already
compiled M0 identity gives

```text
fullLineError(c) = unitIntervalError(c) + m^2,
```

and on `(1,infinity)` the finite sum is exactly `m/x`. Splitting the zero Mellin transform at one
therefore gives an exact identity between `1/s`, the local Mellin transform of the approximation
error on `(0,1)`, and `m/(1-s)`.

The local integral is bounded by Cauchy-Schwarz because `Re(s)>1/2`; its squared weight norm is
finite. The tail term is bounded by `|m|/|1-s|`. Arbitrarily small full-line error forces both
terms to zero, contradicting `1/s != 0`. This excludes all zeta zeros to the right of the critical
line. The completed-zeta functional equation reflects any nontrivial zero to the left into such a
right-side zero, yielding the exact Mathlib RH predicate.
