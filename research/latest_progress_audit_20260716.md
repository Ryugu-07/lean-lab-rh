## Governance Findings

### 1. Route rotation failed globally

Discovery Protocol V3 says to choose a different route after a campaign closes unless independent
audit supplies new evidence. In practice R5 was repeatedly renewed by changing the test family or
the next internal explicit-formula component. Seventeen R5 attempt logs were added in the audited
range. The campaign boundary therefore did not prevent a same-route long run.

### 2. Internal subedges were counted as bridge reduction

The compact arithmetic preregistration correctly labels the work `FORMALIZATION`, and the resulting
theorem is a classical explicit formula. It nevertheless assigns `expected_hard_gap_delta=1` to a
new W1c1 child edge and closes as `BRIDGE_REDUCED`, while W1/G6, W2/G7, and RH all remain open.
This is useful route infrastructure, but under a canonical RH frontier ledger its
`rh_frontier_delta` is zero.

### 3. Independent audit was underspecified

The logs repeatedly request `INDEPENDENT_AUDIT -> ROUTE_SELECTION`, yet the same fast-running
context continued into another R5 campaign. Clean-context or cross-model independence was not
recorded strongly enough to enforce a genuine route challenge.

### 4. The portfolio is deep but historically narrow

The five named families cover Nyman-Beurling/Baez-Duarte, explicit approximants, Li/Weil,
spectral ideas, and explicit formulae. They do not yet source-map classical mollifier and
zero-density work, de Bruijn-Newman heat flow, arithmetic equivalents, function-field/Frobenius
analogies, the full entire-function/de Branges landscape, zero statistics, or rigorous computation.
Recent isolated sources were therefore easier for the selector to discover than decades-old route
families.

### 5. Verification is strong but not sufficient for target fidelity

TargetChecks and AxiomsAudit are materially better than the original hand-written target ledger.
The forbidden scan is clean in the audited source trees. Lean compilation verifies the formal
statements, but not that a project definition exactly matches every cited source, that a classical
theorem was interpreted with the right test class, or that a new theorem is mathematically novel.
High-value iff and explicit-formula results still require clean-context statement crosswalks.

### 6. Vendored infrastructure must stay separately attributed

The `PrimeNumberTheoremAnd` snapshot records an upstream URL, commit, Apache-2.0 license, and scope.
Future reports must distinguish imported upstream complex-analysis infrastructure from project-new
RH theorem assembly. Line counts must not treat the snapshot as newly discovered mathematics.
