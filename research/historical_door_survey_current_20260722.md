## Survey purpose

The survey is an omission-seeking audit, not a bibliography. For every historical door it must
determine:

1. the exact statement and implication direction relative to RH;
2. the last theorem actually proved by humans;
3. the exact next missing lemma or mechanism;
4. the strongest documented obstruction and its primary source;
5. whether that obstruction is a theorem, counterexample, conditional barrier, missing technique,
   or merely a route that ceased to receive attention;
6. whether the obstruction still survives current mathematics, formal methods, and computational
   tools;
7. whether weakening the failed premise still suffices for RH;
8. whether another historical route supplies the missing input;
9. which mature tools in the target domain have not been aimed at the exact RH edge;
10. whether the route has produced unconditional progress on the RH frontier;
11. formalization fit and machine-task fit;
12. no more than three precise probes that could distinguish a real opening from a repackaging.

The core comparison fields are the present validity of the obstacle, the exact missing object,
and the evidence for an overlooked opening. Ease of formalization is not the ranking objective.
