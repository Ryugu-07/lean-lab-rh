## Primary Source

Luis Baez-Duarte, *A Strengthening of the Nyman-Beurling Criterion for the Riemann Hypothesis*:

- arXiv: `https://arxiv.org/abs/math/0202141`
- inspected PDF: `https://arxiv.org/pdf/math/0202141`
- PDF SHA-256: `3ce4aff466443c71094affc1f8b6f5f0dd36cb4377dc5d2ceddbd2537c1d1819`
- relevant locations: abstract; Section 1 and Theorem 1.1; Section 2.2, especially the definitions of
  `f_epsilon,n` and the two Hilbert-space limit arguments.

The source sets `H = L2(0,infinity)`, takes `chi` to be the characteristic function of `(0,1]`,
and uses the natural family `rho_a(x) = rho(1/(a*x))` for positive natural `a`. It calls `Bnat` the
subspace generated by these kernels. In Section 2.2, convergence of finite kernel sums to
`f_epsilon` is used to infer membership in `Bnat`; consequently the generated subspace in Theorem
1.1 is the closed linear span, not the algebraic finite span alone.

The RH direction in the source uses finite coefficients `mu(a) / a^epsilon` with real positive
`epsilon`, so it already produces real approximants. Batch 05 additionally proves that a complex
scalar interpretation gives the same closure condition for the real target and generators.

## Coefficient-Field Proof

`baezDuarteOfRealLp` embeds real `Lp` continuously into complex `Lp`, while
`baezDuarteRealPartLp` takes pointwise real parts continuously. Lean proves:

```text
baezDuarteRealPartLp (baezDuarteOfRealLp f) = f
```

For a complex coefficient `z` and real-valued kernel `rho_n`, Lean also proves:

```text
Re(z * rho_n) = Re(z) * rho_n
```

at the packaged `Lp` level. Therefore the real-part map sends the complex finite span into the real
finite span. Continuity sends complex closure membership back to real closure membership; the
continuous real embedding gives the converse.
